What is this ? Aren't videogames just frenetic zombie shooting sessions ?

They are not, my dear: they are just a medium. To put it simply, the creators are telling a story and the player is giving it an audience. It's exactly like reading: the best book in the world is meaningless if no one reads it.
Shooting zombies is a story as well, just the most brain-dead one possible (pun intended); unfortunately, it's also the one that sells the most.
Still not convinced ? Let me propose some concrete examples:

The Immersive SIM Genre

This kind of videogame aims to give you agency: you think, according to your knowledge of the real world, that using a bomb to break a wall is a possibility to keep chasing the bad guy ? Try it ! You'd rather run to the helicopter to have an advantage ? This is also possible. The world reacts to your actions and you react to it: this is why they are called SIMulators !
You are taking active decisions all the time, at your own pace and according to your current abilities and possibilities and dealing with the consequences of your actions: this is not a brain-dead activity, it's actually the opposite and many employers usually use these exact words on some job postings !
